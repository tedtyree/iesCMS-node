Weight loss greatly reduces the risk of many serious health conditions as well as adds to the longevity of your pets quality of life. The recovery time of many pets after orthopedic surgery is greatly lengthened if their body is forced to bear too much weight.<br />&nl;<br />&nl;The main components of weight loss management include both exercise and nutrition. Medical uses include the use of both surgical lasers (hot lasers) and therapy lasers (cold lasers).</p>&nl;<p>&amp;nbsp;</p>&nl;<p><span style="font-size: 12px;"><span style="font-family: Tahoma;"> </span></span></p>&nl;<p><strong>What is low level LASER Therapy?</strong> Low Level LASER Therapy, commonly known as LLLT (also cold laser or soft laser) is a form of phototherapy which involves the application of monochromatic and coherent light to injuries and lesions to stimulate healing.<br />&nl;<br />&nl;<strong>What is LLLT used for?</strong> LLLT is used to increase the speed, quality and strength of tissue repair, resolve inflammation and give pain relief. Therefore, there are no risks of heat or thermal burns from cold lasers. Photons enter the tissue and are absorbed in the mitochondria and at the cell membrane by photoreceptive molecules called chromophores.
